Foam roll — quads & outer hip

60 sec per area — pause on tender spots

Tap for cues

Start with quads: lie face down, roller under one thigh, slowly roll from hip to knee. Pause on any tender spot for 5–10 seconds.

Then outer hip (TFL/IT band): lie on your side, roller just below the hip bone, work slowly toward the knee.

Avoid rolling directly on the inner thigh or groin.

Lacrosse ball — glute & piriformis

30–60 sec per spot — breathe into it

Tap for cues

Sit on the floor, lacrosse ball under one glute. Cross that ankle over the opposite knee to open up the piriformis. Find a tender spot and breathe — let your bodyweight sink in. Work 2–3 spots per side.

Step 2 — mobility then this
3

Supine lumbar mobility sequence

Hold each position 20–30 sec

Tap for cues

1. Knees to chest — hold 30 sec, gentle rock side to side.

2. Supine rotation — knees together, drop to one side, hold 20–30 sec. Let gravity do the work. Repeat other side.

3. Figure-4 — ankle over opposite knee, gentle pull toward chest, 30 sec each side.

Step 3 — week 1 exercises daily
4

Isometric adductor squeeze

3 × 10 sec holds — moderate effort only

Tap for cues

Lie on back, knees bent, rolled towel between knees. Squeeze at 50–60% effort — not max. Zero pain. If you feel anything at the groin, reduce effort further.
Effort:
5

Side-lying adductor raise

3 × 12 reps each side — 3 sec lowering phase

Tap for cues

Lie on side, bottom leg straight, top leg bent with foot flat in front. Raise bottom leg 6–8 inches, hold 1 sec, lower slowly over 3 seconds. Keep hips stacked. Pain-free range only.
Effort:
6

Single-leg glute bridge

3 × 10 reps each side — squeeze glute first, then lift

Tap for cues

On your back, one knee bent with foot close to glutes, other leg extended hovering in line with your thigh at the top.

Squeeze the glute before you lift. Drive through the heel. Only rise until hips are level — don't hyperextend.

If hamstring cramps: move foot closer to your glutes. If it persists, do double-leg bridges until glute firing improves.
Effort:
Step 4 — week 2 exercises every other day
7

Standing banded adduction

3 × 12 reps each side — lightest band

Tap for cues

Anchor a light band at ankle height. Stand sideways, band around working ankle. Draw leg across body in front, control the return slowly. Stay upright. Lightest band available.
Effort:
8

Copenhagen plank — short lever

3 × 8 sec holds each side — build to 20 sec

Tap for cues

Lie on side, bottom ankle on a low surface (chair seat). Support on forearm. Lift hips — straight line from head to foot, bottom adductor doing the work. Short lever only until fully pain-free.

Step 5 — return to running when ready
9

Run-walk protocol

Day 1: 5 min walk / 10 min jog / 5 min walk

Tap for progression

Gate: Copenhagen plank pain-free for 3 consecutive sessions before starting.

Day 1: 5 min walk → 10 min easy jog → 5 min walk.

Assess the next morning. If pain-free: Day 3 do 20 min continuous easy jog. If not: return to week 2 for 3 more days.

The morning pain reading is your primary signal.
Effort:
Stop any exercise that reproduces inner thigh or groin symptoms. Mild awareness is acceptable — pain is not.
