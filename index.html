Isometric adductor squeeze

3 × 10 sec holds — moderate effort only

Tap for cues

Lie on back, knees bent, rolled towel between knees. Squeeze at 50–60% effort — not max. Zero pain. Loads the tissue without movement, begins rebuilding tensile tolerance. If you feel anything at the groin, reduce effort further.
2

Side-lying adductor raise

3 × 12 reps each side — 3 sec lowering phase

Tap for cues

Lie on side, bottom leg straight, top leg bent with foot flat in front. Raise bottom leg 6–8 inches, hold 1 sec, lower slowly over 3 seconds. Keep hips stacked. Pain-free range only — stop short of any groin discomfort.
3

Single-leg glute bridge

3 × 10 reps each side — squeeze and hold 1 sec

Tap for cues

On your back, one knee bent, other leg extended. Drive through heel, raise hips, squeeze glute at top. Builds hip stability that reduces adductor demand during running gait. Essential — don't skip it.
4

Supine lumbar mobility sequence

Once daily — hold each position 20–30 sec

Tap for cues

Three movements in sequence:

1. Knees to chest — gentle rock side to side.
2. Supine rotation — knees together, drop to one side, hold, repeat.
3. Figure-4 stretch — ankle over opposite knee, gentle pull.

Addresses the lumbar/SI tightness contributing to the overall picture.
Week 2 — every other day load progression
5

Standing banded adduction

3 × 12 reps each side — lightest band

Tap for cues

Anchor a light band at ankle height. Stand sideways, band around working ankle. Draw leg across body in front, control the return slowly. Stay upright. Lightest band available — activation work, not strength training yet.
6

Copenhagen plank — short lever

3 × 8 sec holds each side — build to 20 sec

Tap for cues

Lie on side, bottom ankle on a low surface (chair seat). Support on forearm. Lift hips — straight line, bottom adductor doing the work. Short lever only (ankle, not knee on surface) until fully pain-free. Highest-value exercise in the sequence for running return.
Week 3 — return to running impact reintroduction
7

Run-walk protocol

Day 1: 5 min walk / 10 min jog / 5 min walk

Tap for progression

Gate: Copenhagen plank pain-free for 3 sessions before starting this.

Day 1: 5 min walk warmup → 10 min easy jog → 5 min walk cooldown.

Assess next morning. If pain-free: Day 3 do 20 min continuous easy jog. If not: return to week 2 for 3 more days.

The morning-after response is the real test — not how you feel during the run.
Stop any exercise that reproduces inner thigh or groin symptoms. Mild awareness is acceptable — pain is not. If symptoms increase after a session, drop back one phase.
